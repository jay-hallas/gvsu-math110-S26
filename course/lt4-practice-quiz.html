    Worksheet Learning Target 4: Linear Graphs
    

    Objectives

    • Given a linear graph, identify the slope in the context, the vertical intercept, horizontal intercept, evaluate \(f(a)\text{,}\) find \(x\) given \(f(a)\) and make the equation of the line.
      🔗
    🔗

    1. Reading a Line.

    Use the graph of the line \(y = f(x)\) below to answer the questions. Read your values directly from the grid.
    🔗
    
    A straight line on a coordinate grid with x from negative 1 to 7 and y from negative 5 to 7. The line passes through the points (0, negative 3), (2, 0), (4, 3), and (6, 6), rising 3 units for every 2 units it moves to the right.
    🔗

    (a)

    Find the slope of the line.
    🔗
    Answer.
    Between \((0,-3)\) and \((2,0)\) the line rises \(3\) and runs \(2\) , so the slope is \(\frac{3}{2}\) .
    🔗
    🔗
    🔗

    (b)

    Find the vertical intercept.
    🔗
    Answer.
    The line crosses the \(y\)-axis at \((0,-3)\text{,}\) so the vertical intercept is \(-3\text{.}\)
    🔗
    🔗
    🔗

    (c)

    Find the horizontal intercept.
    🔗
    Answer.
    The line crosses the \(x\)-axis at \((2,0)\text{,}\) so the horizontal intercept is \(2\text{.}\)
    🔗
    🔗
    🔗

    (d)

    Evaluate \(f(4)\text{.}\)
    🔗
    Answer.
    At \(x=4\) the line passes through \((4,3)\text{,}\) so \(f(4)=3\text{.}\)
    🔗
    🔗
    🔗

    (e)

    Find the value of \(x\) for which \(f(x)=6\text{.}\)
    🔗
    Answer.
    The line reaches a height of \(6\) at the point \((6,6)\text{,}\) so \(f(x)=6\) when \(x=6\text{.}\)
    🔗
    🔗
    🔗

    (f)

    Write the equation of the line.
    🔗
    Answer.
    Using the slope \(\frac{3}{2}\) and vertical intercept \(-3\) , the equation is \(y = \frac{3}{2}x - 3\) .
    🔗
    🔗
    🔗
    🔗

    2. Reading Another Line.

    Use the graph of the line \(y = f(x)\) below to answer the questions. Read your values directly from the grid.
    🔗
    
    A straight line on a coordinate grid with x from negative 1 to 6 and y from negative 1 to 9. The line passes through the points (0, 8), (1, 6), (2, 4), (3, 2), and (4, 0), falling 2 units for every 1 unit it moves to the right.
    🔗

    (a)

    Find the slope of the line.
    🔗
    Answer.
    Between \((0,8)\) and \((1,6)\) the line falls \(2\) and runs \(1\) , so the slope is \(-2\) .
    🔗
    🔗
    🔗

    (b)

    Find the vertical intercept.
    🔗
    Answer.
    The line crosses the \(y\)-axis at \((0,8)\text{,}\) so the vertical intercept is \(8\text{.}\)
    🔗
    🔗
    🔗

    (c)

    Find the horizontal intercept.
    🔗
    Answer.
    The line crosses the \(x\)-axis at \((4,0)\text{,}\) so the horizontal intercept is \(4\text{.}\)
    🔗
    🔗
    🔗

    (d)

    Evaluate \(f(1)\text{.}\)
    🔗
    Answer.
    At \(x=1\) the line passes through \((1,6)\text{,}\) so \(f(1)=6\text{.}\)
    🔗
    🔗
    🔗

    (e)

    Find the value of \(x\) for which \(f(x)=2\text{.}\)
    🔗
    Answer.
    The line is at height \(2\) at the point \((3,2)\text{,}\) so \(f(x)=2\) when \(x=3\text{.}\)
    🔗
    🔗
    🔗

    (f)

    Write the equation of the line.
    🔗
    Answer.
    Using the slope \(-2\) and vertical intercept \(8\text{,}\) the equation is \(y = -2x + 8\text{.}\)
    🔗
    🔗
    🔗
    🔗

    3. Renting an Event Venue.

    An event venue charges a fixed booking fee plus an hourly rate for the time you reserve. The graph shows the total cost \(C\) of renting the venue for \(t\) hours.
    🔗
    
    A straight line on a grid with time in hours on the horizontal axis (0 to 8) and total cost in dollars on the vertical axis (0 to 1600). The line begins at 400 dollars when the time is 0 hours and rises steadily, passing through (4, 1000) and reaching 1600 dollars at 8 hours.
    🔗

    (a)

    What is the venue’s hourly rate? Explain how the slope of the line gives this rate.
    🔗
    Answer.
    The slope is the rate of change of cost with respect to time. Reading two points that fall on the grid, from \((0,400)\) to \((4,1000)\) the cost rises \(\$600\) over \(4\) hours, a slope of \(\frac{600}{4}=150\) . So the venue charges \(\$150\) per hour.
    🔗
    🔗
    🔗

    (b)

    Find the vertical intercept. What does it represent in this situation?
    🔗
    Answer.
    The line meets the vertical axis at \((0,400)\) , so the vertical intercept is \(\$400\) . This is the fixed booking fee charged for \(0\) hours of rental—the cost before any hourly charges are added.
    🔗
    🔗
    🔗

    (c)

    Evaluate \(f(4)\) and explain what it tells you.
    🔗
    Answer.
    At \(t=4\) hours the line is at \((4,1000)\) , so \(f(4)=1000\) . Renting the venue for \(4\) hours costs \(\$1000\) .
    🔗
    🔗
    🔗

    (d)

    You have a budget of \(\$1300\text{.}\) Find the value of \(t\) for which \(f(t)=1300\text{,}\) and explain what it means.
    🔗
    Answer.
    The line reaches \(\$1300\) at the point \((6,1300)\) , so \(f(t)=1300\) when \(t=6\) . With \(\$1300\) you can rent the venue for \(6\) hours.
    🔗
    🔗
    🔗

    (e)

    Write an equation for the total cost \(C\) of renting the venue for \(t\) hours.
    🔗
    Answer.
    Using the hourly rate \(150\) as the slope and the booking fee \(400\) as the vertical intercept, the equation is \(C = 150t + 400\text{.}\)
    🔗
    🔗
    🔗

    (f)

    Use your equation to find the horizontal intercept. Does it make sense in this situation?
    🔗
    Answer.
    The horizontal intercept is where the cost is \(0\) . Setting \(150t + 400 = 0\) gives \(t = -\frac{400}{150} = -\frac{8}{3} \approx -2.7\) hours. A negative time has no meaning here, and the cost is never actually \(\$0\) (the booking fee alone is \(\$400\) ), so the horizontal intercept does not make sense in this context.
    🔗
    🔗
